Old School Mac n' Cheese

Description

This is a completely unpretentious, down-home macaroni and cheese recipe just like my grandma and mom always made. A simple white sauce where you add cheese and a few other flavors with macaroni and a potato chip crust. This recipe makes a lot, I like to make two pans so I can take one to a pot luck and have the other one to keep in the fridge for later. It's an easy recipe to adjust down if you only want to make one pan. If you aren't cooking it right away, just keep the topping separate until you are ready to put it in the oven.

Ingredients

  • 1 ¾ pounds whole-wheat macaroni
  • ¾ cup butter
  • ¾ cup all-purpose flour
  • 6 cups milk, divided
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon mustard powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • Salt and ground black pepper to taste
  • 1 (8 ounce) package shredded Cheddar cheese, divided
  • 3 (8 ounce) packages shredded American cheese
  • 1 (8 ounce) bag potato chips (such as Lay's®), crushed
  • 1 cup shredded Cheddar cheese
  • ⅓ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Butter-flavored cooking spray

Steps for making Old-school-mac-and-cheese

  1. Preheat oven to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C).
  2. Boil elbow macaroni in salted water for 8 minutes; drain.
  3. Melt butter, add flour, whisk until brown, about 5 minutes.
  4. Add 1 cup milk, whisk for 45 seconds, repeat twice. Then add 3 cups milk, Worcestershire sauce, mustard powder, onion powder, and cayenne pepper; season.
  5. Cook sauce on low, whisking, until thickened, about 10 minutes. Add cheeses gradually, stirring until melted.
  6. Combine macaroni with cheese sauce, divide into two 9x13-inch baking dishes.
  7. Mix crushed potato chips, 1 cup Cheddar cheese, and Parmesan; top macaroni. Spray with cooking spray.
  8. Bake until golden and bubbling, 35-45 minutes.
